After Yusuf forgave his brothers, the final pieces moved quickly. He sent his shirt with them, and by Allah's permission Ya'qub's sight was restored. The family that had been scattered by jealousy and grief was gathered again.

> "And he raised his parents upon the throne, and they fell before him in prostration. And he said, 'O my father, this is the interpretation of my dream of before. My Lord has made it reality...'"
> - Surah Yusuf, 12:100

That childhood dream had crossed wells, caravans, households, prisons, courts, and years of famine before reaching its meaning. Allah fulfilled it exactly, but only after turning Yusuf into the kind of servant who could receive fulfillment without arrogance.

And then comes one of the most moving endings in the Quran. Yusuf does not stop at reunion. He does not stop at power. He turns to Allah.

> "My Lord, You have given me of sovereignty and taught me of the interpretation of events... Cause me to die a Muslim and join me with the righteous."
> - Surah Yusuf, 12:101

That is how the surah closes: not with self-congratulation, but with worship. Not with a man admiring his own resilience, but with a prophet reading his whole life through the generosity of Allah.

The dream came true. But the deeper victory was that Yusuf reached the end with gratitude intact.
