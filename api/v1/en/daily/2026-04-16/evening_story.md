Yusuf عليه السلام chose integrity, but integrity didn't save him from prison. When the affair became known, when the women of the city began gossiping about the minister's wife, she turned the accusation back on Yusuf. And to clear her own name, she had him imprisoned.

Imagine the injustice of it. He had done nothing wrong. He had chosen Allah over desire, principle over ease. And his reward was a cell.

> ﴿ ثُمَّ بَدَتْ لَهُم مِّن بَعْدِ مَا رَأَوُا الْآيَاتِ لَيَسْجُنُنَّهُ حَتَّىٰ حِينٍ ﴾
> *Thumma badat lahum min ba'di ma ra'aw al-ayat la-yasjunannahu hatta heen*
> "Then it appeared to them after they had seen the signs that he should be imprisoned until a certain time."
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:35

Prison. For years, perhaps. We don't know exactly how long, but long enough that the narrative shifts, that time passes and seasons change. And yet, in that darkness, something remarkable happened. Two fellow prisoners came to him with dreams of their own.

One dreamed of pressing wine. The other of bread and birds. And they came to Yusuf — the dreamer, the interpreter, the one who understood that dreams are messages from Allah.

> ﴿ يَا صَاحِبَيِ السِّجْنِ أَمَّا أَحَدُكُمَا فَيَسْقِي رَبَّهُ خَمْرًا ۖ وَأَمَّا الْآخَرُ فَيُصْلَبُ فَتَأْكُلُ الطَّيْرُ مِن رَّأْسِهِ ﴾
> *Ya sahibayi as-sijni amma ahadukuma fa-yasqi rabbahu khamran wa amma al-akharu fa-yuslabu fa-ta'kulu at-tayru min ra'sihi*
> "O my two companions of prison, as for one of you, he will give drink to his master of wine; but as for the other, he will be crucified, and birds will eat from his head..."
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:41

He interpreted their dreams. Even in prison, his gift was alive. Even in the darkest place, light found a way through him.
