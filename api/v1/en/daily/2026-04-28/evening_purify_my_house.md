The movement of pilgrims we see today rests on a much older command.

> "And [mention] when We designated for Abraham the site of the House, [saying], 'Do not associate anything with Me and purify My House for those who perform tawaf and those who stand [in prayer] and those who bow and prostrate.'"
> - Surah Al-Hajj, 22:26

Before there were crowds, routes, and seasons, there was tawhid. Before there was arrival, there was purification. Allah tied the House to worship, not spectacle.

That matters in these early Hajj preparation days. As hearts turn toward the city of the Prophet ﷺ and the roads that eventually lead onward, the first lesson is not logistics. It is clearing space for Allah.

A heart preparing for sacred travel should ask a simple question: what needs to leave me before I ever arrive? Pride, hurry, harshness, distraction, showing off. The House was purified for worshippers. Our hearts should learn the same work.
