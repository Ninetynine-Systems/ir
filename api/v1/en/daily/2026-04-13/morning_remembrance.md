I woke up this morning thinking about beginnings. How every journey starts with a single step, a single breath, a single moment of intention. The dawn light was just beginning to filter through when I remembered the words of our Messenger ﷺ about consistent deeds — the ones that don't burn bright and fast, but settle quietly into the rhythm of your life.

> ﴿ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الْمُحْسِنِينَ ﴾
> *Innallaha yuhibbul-muhsineen*
> "Indeed, Allah loves those who do good."
> — Al-Baqarah, 2:195

The Prophet ﷺ taught us that Allah loves consistent deeds, even small ones. Not the grand gestures that capture attention for a moment and then fade, but the quiet practices that become part of who you are. A single ayah of Quran after Fajr, read slowly, with a moment of thought after. A moment of gratitude before sleep — naming one thing, just one, that you're thankful for today. These small anchors hold us steady when everything else shifts.

There's something beautiful about how these practices don't demand much of you. They ask for a few minutes, a few words, a small intention. And over time, they accumulate into something you can feel — a kind of steadiness, a gentleness of heart, a way of moving through the world that feels more rooted.

What small thing might you choose today — not to prove anything, not to meet some standard, but simply because it brings you a little closer to Allah?
