There's a story I want to walk through with you over the coming days — the story of Prophet Yusuf عليه السلام. It's a story that unfolds slowly, like the opening of a flower, each petal revealing something new.

It begins with a dream. Yusuf was young — perhaps no more than a child — when he saw eleven stars, the sun, and the moon bowing before him. Imagine waking from such a vision, the images still vivid, the feeling of it lingering on your skin. He went to his father Ya'qub عليه السلام, a prophet himself, and shared what he had seen.

> ﴿ إِذْ قَالَ يُوسُفُ لِأَبِيهِ يَا أَبَتِ إِنِّي رَأَيْتُ أَحَدَ عَشَرَ كَوْكَبًا وَالشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ رَأَيْتُهُمْ لِي سَاجِدِينَ ﴾
> *Idh qala Yusufu li-abihi ya abati inni raaytu ahada 'ashara kawkaban wash-shamsa wal-qamara raaytuhum li sajideen*
> "When Yusuf said to his father, 'O my father, indeed I have seen eleven stars and the sun and the moon; I saw them prostrating to me.'"
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:4

His father recognized something in this dream — something divine, something that would unfold over years, not days. He warned Yusuf not to share it with his brothers. Some things need time to mature before they're spoken.

What dreams have you held close? The ones that feel too precious, too fragile to share?
