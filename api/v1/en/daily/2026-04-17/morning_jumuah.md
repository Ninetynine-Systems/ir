It's Friday.

The Prophet ﷺ said that on this day there is an hour when no Muslim asks Allah for something good except that He gives it. Scholars differ on when exactly — some say between the two khutbahs, some say the last hour before Maghrib.

But the point is: today is different. The whole day carries a weight.

> ﴿ يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا نُودِيَ لِلصَّلَاةِ مِن يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ فَاسْعَوْا إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ ﴾
> *Ya ayyuhalladhiina aamanu idhaa nudiya lis-salaati min yawmil-jumu'ati fas'aw ilaa dhikrillah*
> "O you who have believed, when the adhan is called for the prayer on the day of Jumu'ah, then proceed to the remembrance of Allah."
> — Al-Jumu'ah, 62:9

Try to catch at least some of the khutbah today, even if you're working. Even if it's just a few minutes of your lunch break, spent in quiet dhikr.

May your Jumu'ah be blessed. May the doors of duaa swing wide open for you today.

What do you want to ask Him?
