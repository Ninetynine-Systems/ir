The sun has risen on a new day, a new month, and a blessed Friday. Dhul-Qa'dah has begun — one of the four sacred months, honored since the time of the Prophet ﷺ and before. And it begins on Jumu'ah, the best day of the week.

> ﴿ إِنَّ عِدَّةَ الشُّهُورِ عِندَ اللَّهِ اثْنَا عَشَرَ شَهْرًا فِي كِتَابِ اللَّهِ يَوْمَ خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ مِنْهَا أَرْبَعَةٌ حُرُمٌ ﴾
> *Inna 'iddat ash-shuhoori 'indallahi ithna 'ashara shahran fi kitabillahi yawma khalaqa as-samawati wal-arda minha arba'atun hurum*
> "Indeed, the number of months with Allah is twelve months in the book of Allah on the day He created the heavens and the earth; of these, four are sacred..."
> — At-Tawbah, 9:36

The Prophet ﷺ said about this day: "The best day on which the sun has risen is Friday. On it Adam was created, on it he was entered into Paradise, on he was expelled from it, and the Hour will not be established except on Friday."

What a convergence — sacred month and sacred day meeting at the threshold. If you're able, take a longer bath today. Wear your best clothes. Read Surah Al-Kahf. Send abundant blessings upon the Prophet ﷺ. And prepare for the khutbah with a heart ready to receive.

May Allah bless your Friday and bless your entry into this sacred month.
