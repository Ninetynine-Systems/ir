Years passed in that prison. The fellow prisoner who had promised to remember Yusuf عليه السلام had forgotten, as humans do. But Allah does not forget.

One night, the King of Egypt had a dream that troubled him deeply — seven fat cows being eaten by seven lean cows, and seven green ears of grain being consumed by seven dry ones. He called his advisors and interpreters, but none could make sense of it.

And then, the prisoner who had been saved remembered. "I will inform you of its interpretation," he said. "But let me go to Yusuf in prison. He is the one who knows."

> ﴿ يُوسُفُ أَيُّهَا الصِّدِّيقُ أَفْتِنَا فِي سَبْعِ بَقَرَاتٍ سِمَانٍ يَأْكُلُهُنَّ سَبْعٌ عِجَافٌ ﴾
> *Yusufu ayyuha as-siddeequ aftina fi sab'i baqaratin simanin ya'kuluhunna sab'un 'ijafun*
> "Yusuf, O truthful one, give us your opinion about seven fat cows being eaten by seven lean ones..."
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:46

Notice how they addressed him — "O truthful one." His integrity had been tested, his patience proven. His reputation preceded him even in prison.

And Yusuf, in that moment of opportunity, didn't simply interpret the dream. He offered a comprehensive solution — gather grain during the years of plenty, store it carefully, prepare for the years of famine that would follow.

The King was impressed. And Yusuf saw, finally, the door opening.

> ﴿ أَخْرِجُوهُ مِنَ السِّجْنِ ﴾
> *Ukhrujoohu mina as-sijni*
> "Bring him out from the prison..."
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:50

The prison had been preparation. The darkness had been a womb. And now, at the right moment, the door opened.
