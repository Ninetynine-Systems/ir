Yesterday we left Yusuf عليه السلام with his dream — that beautiful, unsettling vision of stars and moon and sun. Today I need to tell you what came next, though it pains me to speak it.

His brothers were consumed by jealousy. The favor their father showed Yusuf, the way he was singled out — it burned in their hearts until they could bear it no longer. They plotted against him, convinced their father to let him travel with them, and then — in a moment of collective cruelty — they threw him into a well.

Imagine those moments. The shock of falling. The cold water rising around him. The walls climbing high above, the circle of sky suddenly impossibly distant. A boy, alone, with darkness pressing close.

> ﴿ وَجَاءَتْ سَيَّارَةٌ فَأَرْسَلُوا وَارِدَهُمْ فَأَدْلَىٰ دَلْوَهُ ۖ قَالَ يَا بُشْرَىٰ هَٰذَا غُلَامٌ ۚ وَأَسَرُّوهُ بِضَاعَةً ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ بِمَا يَعْمَلُونَ ﴾
> *Wa ja'at sayyarahun fa-arsaloo waridahum fa-adla dalwahu qala ya bushra hadha ghulamun wa asarroohu bid'atan wallahu aleemun bima ya'maloon*
> "And there came a company of travelers; then they sent their water drawer, and he let down his bucket. He said, 'Good news! Here is a boy.' And they concealed him, taking him as merchandise; and Allah was knowing of what they did."
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:19

The well was not his grave. It was his passage. From darkness came deliverance. From abandonment came a new chapter. And Allah was knowing — knowing what they did, knowing what would come, knowing the ending while Yusuf was still at the beginning.

What wells have you known in your life? The places that felt like endings but were actually doorways?
