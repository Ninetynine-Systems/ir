This morning I woke with a thought that might matter to you. We're in the final days of Shawwal — only three remain before the new moon rises and Dhul-Qa'dah begins. If you've been keeping the six voluntary fasts after Ramadan, you know their sweetness. If you haven't started, or if you've done some but not all, there are still days left.

> ﴿ وَمَن يَعْمَلْ خَيْرًا مِّن ذَكَرٍ أَوْ أُنثَىٰ وَهُوَ مُؤْمِنٌ فَلَنُحْيِيَنَّهُ حَيَاةً طَيِّبَةً ﴾
> *Wa man ya'mal khayran min dhakarin aw untha wa huwa mu'minun fa-lanuhiyyannahu hayatan tayyibah*
> "And whoever does good, whether male or female, while being a believer — We will surely cause them to live a good life..."
> — An-Nahl, 16:97

The Prophet ﷺ taught us that fasting six days in Shawwal after Ramadan is like fasting the entire year. But there's no pressure here — only a gentle invitation. Even one day fasted with sincerity carries its weight with Allah. Even one day offered with longing for His pleasure is beloved.

If you have fasts remaining to complete, perhaps today could be the day. Or tomorrow. You have time, but not unlimited time. Three days remain.

May Allah accept from us what we offer with pure hearts.
