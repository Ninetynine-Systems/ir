Tuesday morning.

We're near the end of Shawwal now — three days left in this month. I was thinking about those who had hoped to fast the six days of Shawwal this year and perhaps haven't completed them yet.

If that's you, there's still time. Not much, but enough.

> ﴿ مَنْ صَامَ رَمَضَانَ ثُمَّ أَتْبَعَهُ سِتًّا مِنْ شَوَّالٍ كَانَ كَصِيَامِ الدَّهْرِ ﴾
> *Man sama Ramadana thumma atba'ahu sittan min Shawwali kana ka-siyamid-dahr*
> "Whoever fasts Ramadan and then follows it with six days of Shawwal, it is as if he fasted the entire year."
> — Sahih Muslim, 1164

Three days. Maybe you've been meaning to. Maybe life got loud and the days slipped by. It happens.

If you have three days left in you, these next ones could count. And if you don't — if your body or your circumstances say no — then may Allah accept your intention and your Ramadan, fully and completely.
