The caravan found Yusuf عليه السلام in the well and pulled him out, but his story didn't turn to comfort. Instead, they sold him as merchandise — a handsome young boy fetching a good price in the slave markets of Egypt.

Think of what that journey must have been like. The well behind him, yes, but now chains, unfamiliar faces, a language he didn't understand. From the bottom of a well to the bottom of a market — one darkness traded for another, or so it seemed.

He was purchased by a wealthy man named Al-Aziz, the king's minister. And here, in this household, the tests truly began. The minister's wife saw something in Yusuf — his beauty, certainly, but also his nobility, his quiet dignity. And she tried to tempt him.

> ﴿ وَرَاوَدَتْهُ الَّتِي هُوَ فِي بَيْتِهَا عَن نَّفْسِهِ وَغَلَّقَتِ الْأَبْوَابَ وَقَالَتْ هَيْتَ لَكَ ﴾
> *Wa rawadathu allati huwa fi baytiha 'an nafsihi wa ghallaqati al-abwaba wa qalat hayta laka*
> "And she, in whose house he was, sought to seduce him. She closed the doors and said, 'Come, you.'"
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:23

But Yusuf refused. Not because he didn't feel the pull of human desire — he was human, after all — but because he saw something larger. He saw the door of his Lord.

> ﴿ قَالَ مَعَاذَ اللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّهُ رَبِّي أَحْسَنَ مَثْوَايَ ۖ إِنَّهُ لَا يُفْلِحُ الظَّالِمُونَ ﴾
> *Qala ma'adhallah innahu rabbi ahsana mathwaya innahu la yuflihu az-zalimoon*
> "He said, 'I seek refuge in Allah. Indeed, he is my master, who has made good my residence. Indeed, wrongdoers will not succeed.'"
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:23

Integrity in the dark. That's what this story teaches us. When no one would know, he chose Allah.
