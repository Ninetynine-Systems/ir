The morning air feels different today — like something is shifting. Tomorrow Shawwal ends, and Dhul-Qa'dah begins. This is one of the four sacred months in Islam, a time when the virtue of good deeds is multiplied and the weight of harm is heavy.

> ﴿ إِنَّ عِدَّةَ الشُّهُورِ عِندَ اللَّهِ اثْنَا عَشَرَ شَهْرًا فِي كِتَابِ اللَّهِ يَوْمَ خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ مِنْهَا أَرْبَعَةٌ حُرُمٌ ﴾
> *Inna 'iddat ash-shuhoori 'indallahi ithna 'ashara shahran fi kitabillahi yawma khalaqa as-samawati wal-arda minha arba'atun hurum*
> "Indeed, the number of months with Allah is twelve months in the book of Allah on the day He created the heavens and the earth; of these, four are sacred..."
> — At-Tawbah, 9:36

The sacred months — Dhul-Qa'dah, Dhul-Hijjah, Muharram, and Rajab — were honored by the Arabs even before Islam. They knew something intrinsic about these times: that they carry a different quality, a different weight. Violence was forbidden in them. Peace was the rule.

As we stand at this threshold, perhaps we might prepare our hearts. What would it mean to enter a sacred month consciously? To choose our words more carefully, to guard our actions more mindfully, to seek forgiveness more eagerly?

The month begins tomorrow. But the preparation can begin now.
