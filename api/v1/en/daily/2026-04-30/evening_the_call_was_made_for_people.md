The modern road to Hajj can look technical: visas, flights, waiting lists, documents, buses, gates. The Quran pulls the eye back to the first call.

> "And proclaim to the people the Hajj; they will come to you on foot and on every lean camel; they will come from every distant pass."
> - Surah Al-Hajj, 22:27

That verse is larger than any one century. Every distant route belongs under it. The people keep coming. Across languages, skins, cities, and generations, they keep coming because Allah made the call endure.

When we think of pilgrims preparing for Madinah and the roads of the Hijaz, we are reminded that this ayah keeps unfolding across generations. Even those staying home can still respond in their measure: with longing, dua, reverence, and a heart that says, "Labbayk" before the tongue ever reaches the rite.
