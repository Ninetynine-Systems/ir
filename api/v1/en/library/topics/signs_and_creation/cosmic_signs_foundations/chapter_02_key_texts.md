# Key Texts

## Source anchors

- Surah Ya-Sin 36:38-40
- Surah Al-Mulk 67:3-5

Surah Ya-Sin describes the sun running to its appointed course and the moon passing through measured phases, then reminds us that each moves within its assigned path. The passage teaches order, limit, and submission. The sun and moon are powerful signs, yet they do not escape Allah's decree.

Surah Al-Mulk turns the gaze upward to the layered heavens and challenges the observer to look again. The point is not a hidden code or sensational claim. The point is humility: the vast sky that overwhelms the eye is still a created sign under Allah's control.
