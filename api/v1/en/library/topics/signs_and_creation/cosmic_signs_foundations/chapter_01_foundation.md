# Foundation

## Source anchors

- Surah Yunus 10:5
- Surah Al-Anbiya 21:33

The Quran points the heart to the sun, moon, night, day, and heavens without turning them into objects of worship or speculation. They are signs. Their order teaches that creation is not random in meaning, and that the One who set time, light, and movement in place is the One to whom life returns.

Surah Yunus 10:5 speaks of the sun as radiance and the moon as light, with phases by which people know years and reckoning. Surah Al-Anbiya 21:33 gathers night, day, sun, and moon together as signs moving by Allah's command. A beginner-safe reading starts here: observe, remember, and do not claim more than revelation gives.
