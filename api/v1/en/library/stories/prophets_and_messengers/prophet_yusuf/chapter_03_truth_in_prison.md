# Truth in Prison

## Source anchors

- Surah Yusuf 12:36-57

Prison did not narrow Yusuf's call. Two young men entered with him and spoke of troubling dreams. Before answering, Yusuf first used the moment to speak about Allah, the emptiness of many false lords, and the straight religion passed down through Ibrahim, Ishaq, and Yaqub. Even inside confinement, he remained a servant calling to tawhid.

His interpretations proved true, but Yusuf did not rush to leave prison the first time an opening appeared. Years later, when the king saw a dream none of the court could explain, Yusuf gave the interpretation clearly: years of ease would be followed by years of severe drought, and wise storage would be needed. Yet before accepting release, he asked that the truth about the women be brought into the open.

When the matter was revisited, the women admitted what had happened, and the wife of al-Aziz confessed that Yusuf had spoken the truth. Only then did he come out. The Quran presents this as a restoration of truth before restoration of rank. After that, Yusuf was placed in authority over the storehouses of the land because he was trustworthy and knowledgeable.

This stage shows that patience is not passivity. Yusuf waited, spoke truthfully, and refused a freedom that would have left falsehood standing over his name. Allah joined his innocence, his knowledge, and his patience in one unfolding decree.
