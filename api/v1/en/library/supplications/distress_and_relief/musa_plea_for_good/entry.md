# When To Use It

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Qasas 28:24

This plea comes after Musa helped two women, then withdrew to the shade in a state of need. He did not present demands to Allah or describe a detailed plan for relief. He simply admitted his poverty before the One who provides every good.

That makes this dua fitting for moments of financial need, transition, fatigue, uncertainty, or quiet dependence. It teaches humility more than urgency, and trust more than performance.

No fixed repetition count is attached here. Recite it when you need provision, opening, support, or simply the honesty to admit that every real good comes from Allah.
