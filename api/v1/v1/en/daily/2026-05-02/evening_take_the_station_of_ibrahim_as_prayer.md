The Quran describes the Sacred House as more than a destination.

> "And [mention] when We made the House a place of return for the people and [a place of] security... And take, [O believers], from the standing place of Abraham a place of prayer."
> - Surah Al-Baqarah, 2:125

A place of return. That phrase matters. Human beings keep straying, tiring, hardening, and being called back. The House is tied to return, safety, prayer, and the trace of Ibrahim's obedience.

That is why the Hajj story is never only about distance traveled. It is about being brought back. Some are being brought physically toward the sacred lands now. Others are being brought inward through longing, study, repentance, and prayer.

Both returns are gifts.
