Before Yusuf's childhood dream was fully completed, something else came first: forgiveness.

> ﴿ لَا تَثْرِيبَ عَلَيْكُمُ الْيَوْمَ ۖ يَغْفِرُ اللَّهُ لَكُمْ ﴾
> La tathriba 'alaykumu al-yawm, yaghfirullahu lakum.
> "No blame will there be upon you today. Allah will forgive you."
> - Surah Yusuf, 12:92

That sentence still feels astonishing. Yusuf had every worldly reason to reopen the wound, rehearse the betrayal, and let his brothers sit inside their shame for a while. Instead he chose release.

Forgiveness does not deny that pain was real. It simply refuses to let the pain keep ruling the room.

If there is someone you are not yet ready to forgive, do not force language your heart cannot honestly carry. But you can still ask Allah this morning to loosen the knot a little. To make mercy possible in time. To keep your wound from becoming your identity.
