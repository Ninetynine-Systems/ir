Years passed in that prison. The fellow prisoner who had been saved — the one Yusuf had asked to mention him to the king — forgot about him, as humans do. The mind moves on to the next thing, the next worry, the next relief. For years, Yusuf remained in that cell with no sign of release, no word from the world outside.

But Allah does not forget.

One night, the King of Egypt had a dream that troubled him deeply. He saw seven fat cows being eaten by seven lean cows, and seven green ears of grain being consumed by seven dry ones. The images were vivid, disturbing, and he woke carrying them with him. He called his advisors and interpreters, men who made their living reading dreams and visions, but none could make sense of it.

And then, in that moment of the court's confusion, the prisoner who had been saved remembered. Something clicked in his mind — a name, a face, a conversation from years ago. "I will inform you of its interpretation," he said. "But let me go to Yusuf in prison. He is the one who knows."

> ﴿ يُوسُفُ أَيُّهَا الصِّدِّيقُ أَفْتِنَا فِي سَبْعِ بَقَرَاتٍ سِمَانٍ يَأْكُلُهُنَّ سَبْعٌ عِجَافٌ ﴾
> *Yusufu ayyuha as-siddeequ aftina fi sab'i baqaratin simanin ya'kuluhunna sab'un 'ijafun*
> "Yusuf, O truthful one, give us your opinion about seven fat cows being eaten by seven lean ones..."
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:46

Notice how they addressed him — "O truthful one." That was the word they used for him. His integrity had been tested in that household years ago, and his patience had been proven in the long years of prison. His reputation had preceded him even in confinement. People spoke of him with respect.

And Yusuf, in that moment of opportunity, didn't simply interpret the dream. He offered a comprehensive solution — gather grain during the years of plenty, store it carefully, prepare for the years of famine that would follow. He didn't just read the dream; he gave the king a plan. And then he made a request of his own: send me to gather the stores.

The King was impressed. And Yusuf saw, finally, the door opening.

> ﴿ أَخْرِجُوهُ مِنَ السِّجْنِ ﴾
> *Ukhrujoohu mina as-sijni*
> "Bring him out from the prison..."
> — Surah Yusuf, 12:50

The prison had been preparation. The long years that felt like waiting turned out to be the making of him. When he walked out of that cell, he was not the same boy who had been thrown into the well, nor the same young man who had been imprisoned for choosing integrity. He was someone shaped by years of confinement into patience, by injustice into wisdom, by isolation into dependence on Allah alone.

And now, at the right moment, the door opened.
