When Yusuf's brothers came again, hardship had softened them. This time they did not come with the confidence of men who still imagined they controlled the story. They came in need. They came carrying loss. They came asking for measure and mercy.

> ﴿ فَلَمَّا دَخَلُوا عَلَيْهِ قَالُوا يَا أَيُّهَا الْعَزِيزُ مَسَّنَا وَأَهْلَنَا الضُّرُّ وَجِئْنَا بِبِضَاعَةٍ مُّزْجَىٰةٍ فَأَوْفِ لَنَا الْكَيْلَ وَتَصَدَّقْ عَلَيْنَا ۖ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَجْزِي الْمُتَصَدِّقِينَ ﴾
>
> *Fa-lammā dakhalū ʿalayhi qālū yā ayyuhā l-ʿazīzu massanā wa-ahlanā l-ḍurru wa-ji'nā bi-biḍāʿatin muzjatan fa-awfi lanā l-kayla wa-taṣaddaq ʿalaynā inna l-lāha yajzī l-mutaṣaddiqīn*
>
> "Indeed, adversity has touched us and our family, and we have come with goods of little worth, so give us full measure and be charitable to us..."
> - Surah Yusuf, 12:88

And then the moment came.

Yusuf asked them whether they understood what they had done long ago, when they had acted in ignorance. The room changed. They looked at him again. Something in his words, his bearing, his mercy, opened recognition in them.

> ﴿ قَالُوا أَئِنَّكَ لَأَنتَ يُوسُفُ ۖ قَالَ أَنَا يُوسُفُ وَهَٰذَا أَخِي ۖ قَد مَّنَّ اللَّهُ عَلَيْنَا ۖ إِنَّهُ مَن يَتَّقِ وَيَصْبِرْ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يُضِيعُ أَجْرَ الْمُحْسِنِينَ ﴾
>
> *Qālū a-innaka la-anta yūsufu qāla anā yūsufu wa-hādhā akhī qad manna l-lāhu ʿalaynā innahu man yattaqī wa-yaṣbiru fa-inna l-lāha lā yuḍīʿu ajra l-muḥsinīn*
>
> "They said, 'Are you indeed Yusuf?' He said, 'I am Yusuf, and this is my brother. Allah has certainly been gracious to us. Indeed, whoever fears Allah and is patient, then Allah does not allow the reward of those who do good to be lost.'"
> - Surah Yusuf, 12:90

Can you imagine what that moment felt like? The brother they had envied, wounded, and cast away was standing before them not as a crushed man, but as one upheld by Allah. And the first language on his tongue was not triumph. It was recognition of Allah's grace.

That is one of the purest signs of healing: when a person can name the mercy of Allah before naming the injury of people.
