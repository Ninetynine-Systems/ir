# Humility Before the Promise

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Kahf 18:98-99

After the barrier was completed, Dhul-Qarnayn did not celebrate himself. He said, "This is a mercy from my Lord." Then he immediately tied the whole matter to the future promise of Allah: when that promise comes, the barrier itself will be leveled.

That ending keeps the story from being read as a celebration of worldly control. Even a ruler given unusual reach and unusual means remains under the decree of Allah. His work can be real and beneficial, but it is never final in the face of the Last Day.

So the account closes where it began: not with fascination about hidden history, but with guidance about power. Justice matters. Means matter. Effort matters. Yet the heart of the story is still servanthood, humility, and the certainty that every structure stands only as long as Allah wills.
