# Trial in the House

## Source anchors

- Surah Yusuf 12:21-35

Once Yusuf was brought into Egypt, the man who purchased him told his household to honor his stay. The Quran marks this as part of Allah's plan to establish Yusuf in the land and to teach him the interpretation of events. Even before public power came to him, his Lord was preparing him through hidden stages.

The trial inside the house was moral before it was social. The woman of the house sought to seduce him, and Yusuf answered with istiadhah: he sought refuge in Allah. When the accusation turned against him, Allah brought forward a sign through the testimony of a witness from her people, and the truth of the moment was exposed.

News spread through the city, and the pressure widened. The women who criticized her saw Yusuf and were struck by his beauty, yet Yusuf did not use the attention to secure comfort. He asked Allah for prison rather than yielding to what he was being called toward, because he knew that being protected from sin mattered more than being protected from hardship.

This chapter teaches that preservation is also a gift from Allah. Yusuf did not trust himself; he asked for help. Integrity in the Quran is not loud self-confidence. It is turning to Allah when desire, pressure, and reputation all close in at once.
