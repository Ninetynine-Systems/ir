# Landing and the Lesson Left

## Source anchors

- Surah Hud 11:47-49
- Surah As-Saffat 37:75-82
- Surah Nuh 71:26-28

After the waters subsided, Nuh was taught how to return to Allah with humility. He sought refuge from asking for what he did not know and asked for mercy. The ark did not end in self-congratulation. It ended in repentance, gratitude, and renewed servanthood.

The Quran remembers Nuh as a grateful servant and preserves peace upon him among later generations. His story remains both mercy and warning: mercy for those who hear the message and board by faith, and warning for those who confuse delay with safety.

The life of Nuh leaves behind a steady lesson for every age. A believer may call for a long time, feel the pain of rejection, and still remain under Allah's care from first warning to final landing. The outcome belongs to Allah, but patience, truthfulness, and gratitude are always within the servant's reach.
