# Calling To Tawhid

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-An'am 6:74-83
- Surah Maryam 19:41-50
- Surah Al-Anbiya 21:51-73

The Quran presents Ibrahim عليه السلام as a servant who would not let inherited practice replace the truth of Allah's oneness. He spoke to his father with tenderness and clarity, calling him away from worship that could neither hear nor see nor benefit him.

His argument against false worship was not theatrical invention. The Quran shows him exposing the weakness of idols and turning the eye toward the Lord who created, guides, feeds, and gives life and death. The lesson is steady: tawhid is not only a belief held in private. It is a way of seeing the world truthfully.

Ibrahim's courage did not come from harshness. It came from certainty. He could be gentle with his father and firm against shirk at the same time.
