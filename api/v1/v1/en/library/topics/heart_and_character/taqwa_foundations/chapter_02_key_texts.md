# Key Texts

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:183
- Surah Al-Hujurat 49:13
- Surah At-Talaq 65:2-3

Surah Al-Baqarah 2:183 explains that fasting was prescribed so believers may attain taqwa. This shows that taqwa is not an abstract concept left in the mind alone. Worship trains it. Obedience deepens it. Discipline protects it.

Surah Al-Hujurat 49:13 also teaches that the most honored people before Allah are those with the most taqwa, not those with the strongest lineage, status, or social weight. The measure is inward and moral before it is social.

Surah At-Talaq 65:2-3 links taqwa to divine openings and reliance. The point is not a promise of effortless life. It is a promise that conscious obedience is never wasted before Allah, and that He makes ways out and provisions where the servant could not have planned them alone.
