# Lived Practice

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:177
- Surah At-Talaq 65:2-3

In lived practice, taqwa appears in choices that often seem ordinary: truthful speech, guarded eyes, clean earnings, patient restraint, fulfilled trusts, and quick repentance after slipping. It is not hidden only in private feeling. It becomes visible in what a person does when temptation, anger, or pride appears.

Surah Al-Baqarah 2:177 helps here by showing righteousness in belief, worship, spending, patience, and keeping commitments. Taqwa does not grow by slogans alone. It grows when a believer repeatedly chooses what Allah loves over what the ego prefers.

For beginners, a helpful rule is simple: let awareness of Allah enter the small moments, not only the dramatic ones. Taqwa is built when the heart remembers Allah before the tongue speaks, before the hand takes, and before the self justifies what it already wants.
