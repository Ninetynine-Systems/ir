# Key Texts

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Hajj 22:26-37
- Sahih Muslim 1218
- Jami at-Tirmidhi 889

Surah Al-Hajj 22:26-37 gathers many of the meanings around pilgrimage: the House is purified for worship, the people are called to come for Hajj, and the rites are tied to remembrance of Allah. Even sacrifice is turned back toward taqwa, because neither flesh nor blood reaches Allah, but the piety of the worshipper does.

The Sunnah clarifies the gravity and mercy of the pilgrimage. Sahih Muslim 1218 links an accepted Hajj free of obscenity and wickedness to a return as pure as the day of one's birth, and the prophetic guidance also highlights the standing at Arafah as central to the rite.
