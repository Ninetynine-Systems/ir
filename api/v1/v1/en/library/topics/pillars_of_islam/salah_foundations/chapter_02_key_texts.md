# Key Texts

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:238
- Surah Al-Ankabut 29:45
- Sahih Muslim 614

Surah Al-Baqarah 2:238 commands believers to guard the prayers carefully. The wording teaches more than mere performance. Salah is something to be protected, prioritized, and treated as a trust rather than fitted around every lesser concern.

Surah Al-Ankabut 29:45 also states that prayer restrains indecency and wrongdoing. This does not mean every person who prays becomes instantly transformed, but it does mean that rightly established prayer is meant to reform the heart and conduct over time.

The Sunnah anchors practice to the Prophet's example. In Sahih Muslim 614, he taught, "Pray as you have seen me praying." At the foundations level, this means salah is learned through transmitted form and reverent care, not invented from personal preference.
