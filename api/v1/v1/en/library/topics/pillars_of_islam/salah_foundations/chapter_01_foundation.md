# Foundation

## Source anchors

- Surah Taha 20:14
- Sahih al-Bukhari 1513

Salah is not a secondary act in Islam. It stands among the pillars named in the Sunnah, and the Quran presents it as an act established for the remembrance of Allah. Prayer is therefore both obligation and gift: a commanded worship that repeatedly returns the believer to the Lord throughout the day.

At the foundation level, a beginner should know that the five daily prayers are fixed acts of worship tied to revealed guidance, not a flexible symbol for general spirituality. They gather recitation, standing, bowing, prostration, remembrance, and humility into a form taught by the Prophet.

That is why salah sits near the center of a Muslim life. It is the recurring meeting that interrupts heedlessness, resets intention, and keeps worship from becoming occasional.
