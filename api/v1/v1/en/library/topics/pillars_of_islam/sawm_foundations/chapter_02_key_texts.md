# Key Texts

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:185
- Sahih al-Bukhari 2014
- Sahih al-Bukhari 38
- Sahih Muslim 1161

Surah Al-Baqarah 2:185 frames Ramadan with guidance, ease, and gratitude. Allah commands fasting for those who witness the month, while also making room for illness and travel with compensating days later. Even at the level of obligation, the ayah reminds the believer that Allah intends ease and not hardship.

The Sunnah adds the spiritual promise around the month. In Sahih al-Bukhari 2014, fasting Ramadan with faith and hope of reward is linked to forgiveness. In Sahih al-Bukhari 38, standing in prayer in Ramadan with the same faith and hope is also linked to forgiveness. Sahih Muslim 1161 points the believer toward the last ten nights in the search for Laylat al-Qadr.
