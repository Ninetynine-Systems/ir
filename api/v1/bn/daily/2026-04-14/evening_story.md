Yesterday we left Yusuf عليه السلام with his dream — that beautiful, unsettling vision of stars and moon and sun, and his father's quiet warning to keep it to himself. Today I need to tell you what came next, though it pains me to speak it.

Because the dream was not the beginning of ease. It was the beginning of a long road that would take Yusuf through betrayal, loss, and displacement before it ever reached the place his father had glimpsed.

His brothers were consumed by jealousy. The favor their father showed Yusuf — the way he was singled out, the way Ya'qub's heart seemed to lean toward him — burned in their hearts until they could bear it no longer. They talked among themselves about what to do, and the conversation darkened until they had settled on a plan.

They convinced their father to let Yusuf travel with them. You can almost hear him — a careful father who didn't want to let his son go but was persuaded, as fathers sometimes are, by the persistence of those asking. And then, away from their father's eyes, in a moment of collective cruelty, they threw him into a well.

He was a boy, and the world turned on him without warning. The walls of the well rose above him, the circle of sky growing smaller as his brothers' faces disappeared above. He must have called out at first. Or perhaps he sat quietly, the way some people do when they realize that no one is coming and that whatever happens next is in Allah's hands.

Hours passed. The well was cold and damp. The darkness pressed close. And then came the sound of approaching travelers — a caravan stopping to draw water.

> ﴿ وَجَاءَتْ سَيَّارَةٌ فَأَرْسَلُوا وَارِدَهُمْ فَأَدْلَىٰ دَلْوَهُ ۖ قَالَ يَا بُشْرَىٰ هَٰذَا غُلَامٌ ۚ وَأَسَرُّوهُ بِضَاعَةً ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ بِمَا يَعْمَلُونَ ﴾
> *Wa ja'at sayyarahun fa-arsaloo waridahum fa-adla dalwahu qala ya bushra hadha ghulamun wa asarroohu bid'atan wallahu aleemun bima ya'maloon*
> "And there came a company of travelers; then they sent their water drawer, and he let down his bucket. He said, 'Good news! Here is a boy.' And they concealed him, taking him as merchandise; and Allah was knowing of what they did."
> — সূরা ইউসুফ, 12:19

The water-drawer lowered his bucket and found a boy instead of water. Think of that moment — the surprise, the quickening excitement, the decision made in an instant to keep him secret, to treat him as merchandise. And just like that, Yusuf was pulled from the well and handed into the hands of strangers.
