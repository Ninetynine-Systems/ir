The caravan found Yusuf عليه السلام in the well and pulled him out, but his story didn't turn toward comfort. Instead, they sold him as merchandise — a handsome young boy fetching a good price in the slave markets of Egypt.

He left behind the only home he'd known — the land of his father, the faces he'd grown up with, the language and landscape that shaped his earliest memories. He went as a captive into a foreign place, among people who spoke differently, who dressed differently, who moved through a world he had no map for. Whatever life he'd been living was over. Something else was beginning, though he couldn't have seen what.

The journey itself must have been long and disorienting. Days on the road, the landscape shifting from the familiar hills of Palestine into the flat, bright land of Egypt. The traders moving their goods, and among them a boy who was themselves a good. He must have watched the sky on those nights — the same stars, the same moon — and wondered if his father was looking up at them too.

He was purchased by a wealthy man named Al-Aziz, the king's minister. And here, in this household, the tests truly began.

Yusuf grew into the house. He was given responsibility, trust, a place of standing despite being a foreign slave. The minister's wife saw something in him — his beauty, certainly, but also his nobility, his quiet dignity, the way he carried himself with the kind of grace that comes from a lineage he didn't need to announce. And she was drawn to him.

> ﴿ وَرَاوَدَتْهُ الَّتِي هُوَ فِي بَيْتِهَا عَن نَّفْسِهِ وَغَلَّقَتِ الْأَبْوَابَ وَقَالَتْ هَيْتَ لَكَ ﴾
> *Wa rawadathu allati huwa fi baytiha 'an nafsihi wa ghallaqati al-abwaba wa qalat hayta laka*
> "And she, in whose house he was, sought to seduce him. She closed the doors and said, 'Come, you.'"
> — সূরা ইউসুফ, 12:23

She closed the doors. It was private. No one would know. The opportunity was there, the desire was there, the circumstances aligned — and Yusuf refused.

> ﴿ قَالَ مَعَاذَ اللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّهُ رَبِّي أَحْسَنَ مَثْوَايَ ۖ إِنَّهُ لَا يُفْلِحُ الظَّالِمُونَ ﴾
> *Qala ma'adhallah innahu rabbi ahsana mathwaya innahu la yuflihu az-zalimoon*
> "He said, 'I seek refuge in Allah. Indeed, he is my master, who has made good my residence. Indeed, wrongdoers will not succeed.'"
> — সূরা ইউসুফ, 12:23

Integrity in the dark. That's what this moment teaches us. When no one would know, when the circumstances made it easy, when every human instinct might have whispered just this once — he chose Allah. Not because he didn't feel the pull of desire, but because he felt something larger.
