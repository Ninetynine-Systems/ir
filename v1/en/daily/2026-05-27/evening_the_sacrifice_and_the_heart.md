Eid al-Adha carries us back to the surrender of Ibrahim and Ismail.

> "Indeed, this was the clear trial. And We ransomed him with a great sacrifice."
> - Surah As-Saffat, 37:106-107

The story is not preserved so we can admire sacrifice from a distance. It asks something of us. What do we keep above Allah? What comfort, pride, anger, or habit still resists surrender?

The outward sacrifice has meaning because the heart is being called to surrender. If the day does not soften the heart, we have missed part of its mercy.

Let Eid ask you gently: what is Allah inviting me to place beneath Him?
