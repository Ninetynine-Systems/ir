The Quran tells us why the people come.

> "That they may witness benefits for themselves and mention the name of Allah on known days..."
> - Surah Al-Hajj, 22:28

The benefits are real, but the verse does not leave them undefined for long. It ties the journey back to remembrance. Hajj is not preserved so that the believer can collect impressive memories. It is preserved so that the Name of Allah remains central.

That principle helps even before the rites begin. If these days of movement and preparation are filling your mind, keep asking: is Allah's name staying central in my thoughts, speech, and intention? If not, then something has already drifted.
