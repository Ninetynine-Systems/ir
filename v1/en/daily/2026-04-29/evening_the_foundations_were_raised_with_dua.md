One of the most beautiful scenes in the Hajj story is not triumph, but humility.

> "And [mention] when Abraham was raising the foundations of the House and [with him] Ishmael, [saying], 'Our Lord, accept [this] from us. Indeed, You are the Hearing, the Knowing.'"
> - Surah Al-Baqarah, 2:127

They were doing one of the greatest outward works in sacred history, yet the words on their tongues were not self-congratulation. They were asking for acceptance.

That is a needed correction for us. It is possible to be busy with a religious task and still need to tremble over whether Allah accepts it. Bags can be packed. Routes can be fixed. Plans can be made. But the deeper work is to keep saying, "Our Lord, accept from us."

The builders of the House made dua while they built. We should not be less humble than that.
