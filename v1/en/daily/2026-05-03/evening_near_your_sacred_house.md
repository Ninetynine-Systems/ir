There is a tenderness in Ibrahim's dua that reaches far beyond his own time.

> "Our Lord, I have settled some of my descendants in an uncultivated valley near Your sacred House... so make hearts among the people incline toward them..."
> - Surah Ibrahim, 14:37

The valley was barren, but Allah made hearts incline. That is one of the great signs tied to this story. Material scarcity did not prevent spiritual attraction. Year after year, hearts keep leaning toward that place.

We can see another form of that in this season. People begin preparing for Madinah, for the roads of the Hijaz, and for the coming rites. Long before the body reaches the House, the heart is already being drawn.

If you feel that pull, do not dismiss it as sentiment. It may be one of the gentlest invitations Allah sends.
