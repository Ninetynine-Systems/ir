# Return of the Brothers

## Source anchors

- Surah Yusuf 12:58-87

When famine reached the surrounding lands, Yusuf's brothers came to Egypt seeking provisions. He recognized them, but they did not recognize him. He treated them with measured generosity and told them to return with their younger brother if they wanted a full measure again.

Back with their father, they pressed the matter, and Yaqub only released the younger son after taking a solemn pledge from them. When they returned, Yusuf drew his brother close and quietly told him who he was. Soon after, the king's cup was placed in his brother's bag, and the old wound in the family opened again. The brothers stood in confusion and shame, unable to explain what was unfolding.

When they returned to Yaqub with more loss, his grief deepened, yet his speech remained turned toward Allah. He said that beautiful patience was fitting for him, and he sent them back again to search for Yusuf and his brother without despairing of Allah's relief. The chapter does not hide the ache of waiting, but it never lets grief become hopelessness.

Here the surah teaches a difficult balance: sorrow can be intense, and still trust can remain upright. Yaqub's patience was not the absence of tears. It was the refusal to let pain become disbelief in Allah's mercy.
