# The Long Call

## Source anchors

- Surah Nuh 71:1-20
- Surah Ash-Shu'ara 26:105-115

Nuh was sent to his people with a plain call: worship Allah, fear Him, and obey the messenger sent among you. The Quran presents him as patient and persistent. He spoke by night and by day, openly and privately, returning again and again to the same truth with no need for novelty.

His people answered with resistance. Surah Nuh describes them putting fingers in their ears, covering themselves with their garments, and insisting on arrogance. Their refusal was not born from a lack of clarity. The warning reached them, but they chose pride over surrender.

The story teaches that prophetic patience is not measured by quick results. Nuh continued because the truth was worth carrying even when hearts stayed hard. The believer learns here that sincerity does not depend on applause, and that a long season of calling can still be noble in the sight of Allah.
