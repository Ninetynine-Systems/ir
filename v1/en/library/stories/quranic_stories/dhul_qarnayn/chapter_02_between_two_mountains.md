# Between Two Mountains

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Kahf 18:89-97

Dhul-Qarnayn continued until he came between two barriers where he found a people who could scarcely understand speech. They asked him for help against Ya'juj and Ma'juj and offered payment in return. He declined to make the work a personal gain.

Instead, he said that what Allah had established him in was better, then asked them to help him with strength. Iron was gathered, the gap was filled, and molten copper was poured over it until a barrier stood that the corrupting force could neither scale nor pierce.

The Quran shows both competence and humility in this scene. Dhul-Qarnayn does not pretend the achievement is his alone. He uses the means Allah gave him, invites the people into the work, and answers corruption with organized effort rather than spectacle.
