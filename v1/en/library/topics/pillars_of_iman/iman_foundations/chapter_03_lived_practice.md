# Lived Practice

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:177

At the lived level, iman shows itself in what the Quran keeps linking to it: prayer, charity, truthfulness, patience, and honoring commitments. Faith is inward, but the Quran does not leave it inward only. It is expected to leave traces in worship, speech, endurance, and dealings with people.

This is why a believer returns often to the foundations. When the heart weakens, the answer is not to chase novelty. It is to renew what Allah has already taught: His oneness, His revelation, His promise, His judgment, and His decree. From there the limbs follow.

The beginner's task is steady rather than dramatic: learn the pillars of iman clearly, ask Allah for firmness, and let those beliefs shape ordinary obedience over time.
