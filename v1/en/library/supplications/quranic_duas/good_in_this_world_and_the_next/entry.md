# When To Use It

## Source anchors

- Surah Al-Baqarah 2:201

This is one of the Quran's most complete supplications. It asks for good in this life, good in the Hereafter, and protection from the Fire without narrowing "good" to wealth, comfort, or one immediate result. The request stays broad enough to hold faith, health, lawful provision, useful relationships, and a safe ending.

The verse appears in the Hajj passage of Surah Al-Baqarah, but its wording is not limited to pilgrims alone. It teaches the believer to ask with balance: not for dunya alone, and not for an abstract Hereafter while neglecting present responsibility.

No fixed repetition count is attached here. Recite it as a frequent, balanced dua when you want your requests to stay gathered around what is truly good before Allah.
